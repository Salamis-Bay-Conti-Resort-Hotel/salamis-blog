What Are Souvenirs Unique to Cyprus?

When you start looking around, you will notice that the most special items are the ones that feel connected to the earth and the history here. If you want to know what souvenirs to buy in Cyprus, start with the symbols of the island.

The mouflon, a shy wild sheep found only in our mountains, is a favorite. You can find its image on high-quality ceramics or hand-painted ornaments that look great on any shelf. Copper is another big one. The island was actually the primary source of copper in the ancient world, and you can still find local smiths in Famagusta making beautiful decorative bowls.

Quick tip

The best souvenirs from Cyprus are “conversation pieces”: items that carry a story (a symbol, a craft technique, a local ingredient) and feel truly connected to the island.

  • Mouflon motifs
  • Copper craftsmanship
  • Salamis-inspired patterns
  • Handmade ceramics
  • Olive-wood pieces
  • Local lace & needlework

3 Questions Before You Buy

  1. Is it travel-friendly? Fragile, liquid, strongly scented?
  2. Who is it for? Foodie, decor lover, jewelry person, history buff?
  3. Does it have a story? Ask about origin, technique, or symbol meaning.

Cyprus Handicrafts: A Blend of Tradition and Modernity

The craft scene here is quite surprising. You might expect only old-fashioned items, but the local artists are actually very good at making traditional techniques look modern. Handmade pottery often features designs inspired by Bronze Age artifacts found at the Salamis ruins, but with a sleek finish that fits perfectly in a 21st-century living room.

Handmade Ceramics

Look for clean, modern silhouettes combined with ancient motifs. A small bowl or a mug is the perfect “daily reminder” souvenir.

  • Ask if it’s food-safe if you plan to use it
  • Request protective wrapping for luggage
  • Choose a size that fits your suitcase

Copper Work

Decorative bowls and trays made by local smiths feel timeless. They also age beautifully, which makes them a great long-term keepsake.

  • Ask about care (polishing, storage)
  • Pick flatter pieces for easy packing
  • Look for hand-hammered textures
Style hack

If you want something that blends into modern interiors, choose neutral shapes (simple mugs, small vases, minimal ornaments) with one local motif detail.

Hellim Cheese: The Tasty Souvenir from Cyprus

It is no secret that a huge part of the holiday joy comes from the food. If you are looking to pack the island’s most famous taste into your suitcase, Hellim is a must-buy. This salty, squeaky cheese is widely considered one of the most popular Cyprus traditional souvenirs for a reason.

Luckily, it is also very easy to transport. You can pick up vacuum-sealed packs in almost any local shop that stay perfectly fresh during your flight, making them the ultimate tasty memento.

Buying checklist
  • Choose vacuum-sealed packs for easy travel
  • Check best-before dates and storage instructions
  • Perfect gift for BBQ lovers and mezze fans

Cypriot Olive Oil and Olive Products

The silver-green olive trees you see all over the island are more than just part of the scenery; they are the source of our “liquid gold.” High-quality, cold-pressed olive oil is one of the top Cyprus souvenirs for a reason. It tastes like pure sunshine.

But the olive love does not stop at the oil. You can also find amazing natural soaps that make your skin feel incredible after a day in the sun.

For Foodies

  • Cold-pressed olive oil
  • Olive mixes & tapenade-style spreads
  • Herb-infused oils

For Self-Care

  • Olive oil soaps
  • Body lotions
  • Gift sets with local scents

Travel Notes

  • Liquids go in checked baggage
  • Wrap bottles well
  • Ask for gift-ready packaging

Jewelry and Handmade Designs from Cyprus

If you want something a bit more delicate or romantic, the local jewelry is stunning. Some of the best Cyprus souvenirs come from the silver workshops. There is a style called Telkari, which is a very fine filigree work. Artisans twist thin silver wires into patterns that look like metallic lace.

What to Look For

  • Telkari filigree earrings
  • Minimal silver pendants
  • Pieces inspired by Mediterranean symbols

Gift Tip

  • Minimal pieces are easier to match daily outfits
  • Ask about silver care and storage
  • Handmade certificates/notes add value

Cypriot Needlework: From Tradition to the Future

The local lace here is something else. These Cyprus holiday souvenirs are created by artisans who have spent years perfecting every tiny stitch. The geometric designs are iconic and look amazing, whether they are on a small coaster or a framed piece for your wall.

Who will love it?

Anyone who appreciates slow craft, handmade details, or elegant home textiles. Small sizes (coasters, runners, mini cloths) are the easiest to pack.

Spices and Culinary Preparations from Cyprus

The Cyprus holiday souvenirs found in spice markets are so much more aromatic than what you find in a standard supermarket. Look for wild oregano, which has a very intense scent, or sumac, which adds a lovely lemony zing to salads.

Remember carob syrup, often called “black gold” because it is so rich and beloved. And if you have a sweet tooth, do not forget to grab some Macun.

Top Picks

  • Wild oregano
  • Sumac
  • Herb blends
  • Carob syrup (“black gold”)
  • Macun preserves

How to Gift It

  • Choose labeled jars/pouches (ingredients matter)
  • Add a mini recipe note (salads, grilled meats)
  • Zip bags help avoid scent leakage

Souvenirs You Can Find in Cyprus: Local Shops and Markets

Finding that perfect gift is the other half of a fun journey, isn’t it? If you find something truly authentic, you absolutely need to visit the local spots. Take a short trip to Bandabuliya in Famagusta. You’ll hear the calls of shopkeepers, smell the freshly roasted coffee, and see locals grabbing their daily bread.

Market mindset
  • Go earlier for calmer browsing
  • Ask where it was made (local workshop vs imported)
  • For fragile items, request extra wrapping
