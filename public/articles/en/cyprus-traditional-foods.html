An Overview of Cypriot Cuisine

The culinary world of Cyprus is a blend of Greek, Turkish, Middle Eastern, and Mediterranean traditions. Fresh vegetables, olive oil, herbs, and grilled meats define everyday cooking. What makes Cypriot cuisine special is its simplicity, freshness, and authenticity.

Halloumi Cheese: An Iconic Cypriot Flavor

Halloumi is the island’s most famous cheese. It does not melt when heated, making it perfect for grilling or frying. Its salty flavor and signature squeaky texture make it unforgettable.

Şeftali Kebab: A Flavorful Dish with an Unexpected Name

Made with minced lamb or beef, herbs, and spices wrapped in caul fat, this charcoal-grilled dish is incredibly juicy and deeply flavorful. It is one of the most beloved traditional foods in Cyprus.

Molehiya: A Unique and Nutritious Flavor

Molehiya is prepared from dried jute leaves cooked slowly with meat, garlic, and lemon. It is a comforting dish often served at family gatherings and celebrations.

Cypriot Desserts: The Best of Syrup Sweets

Traditional desserts rely on nuts, semolina, citrus zest, and fragrant syrups. Spoon sweets, baklava, kadaifi, and revani are among the island’s most loved treats.

Pilavuna: Traditional Cypriot Pastry

Pilavuna is a savory-sweet pastry filled with cheese, eggs, mint, and raisins. Traditionally prepared during holidays, it is now enjoyed year-round.

Cyprus’ Unique Breakfast Culture

Breakfast in Cyprus is all about sharing and variety. Tables are filled with olives, fresh vegetables, jams, honey, eggs, and grilled halloumi.

Meze Culture: The Heart of Cypriot Dining

Meze is not just a meal — it is a social experience. Dozens of small dishes are served slowly while conversation flows.

Cyprus' Famous Street Foods

Street foods include lahmacun, pastries, grilled meats, and halloumi sandwiches. Quick, simple, and delicious — they reflect the everyday flavor of island life.
